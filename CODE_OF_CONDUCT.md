# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of Larjango pledge to make participation in our community a respectful and welcoming experience for everyone.

We welcome people of all backgrounds, experience levels, identities, and perspectives.

## Expected Behavior

Community members are expected to:

- be respectful and kind
- welcome newcomers
- give constructive feedback
- focus on ideas, not personal attacks
- accept different opinions and experiences
- help keep discussions productive

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment or discrimination
- insults, threats, or personal attacks
- trolling or deliberately disruptive behavior
- sharing private information without permission
- inappropriate or offensive comments
- repeated unwanted contact

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing community standards.

Maintainers may remove comments, close discussions, reject contributions, or restrict participation when behavior does not follow this Code of Conduct.

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainer through GitHub.

Reports will be reviewed respectfully and privately when possible.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all Larjango community spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, and any future community channels.

## Goal

The goal of Larjango is to build a helpful, respectful, and beginner-friendly open-source community.
